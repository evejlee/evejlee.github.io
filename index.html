Postdoc Lunch Seminar Series
(2017 - 2018)

"Time is an illusion. Lunchtime, doubly so."
DOUGLAS ADAMS

Postdocs in astronomy, astrophysics, cosmology, numerical relativity, and gravitational waves from Caltech and JPL meet once a week for a talk and catered lunch. The idea is to promote stimulating interaction in an informal environment. A designated speaker leads a 20 minute discussion on anything science related — preferably not your own research, but something you find interesting! No slides required. Because of the diverse interests of the participants, the topic should be made accessible to a broad audience.

The Postdoc Lunch Seminars are held Thursdays in Cahill 312 at 12pm, unless otherwise noted.

How to order lunch

Each week there is one person responsible for ordering lunch. If it’s your turn, here are some tips and things you should know:

  • You’ll be reimbursed! See Sheri Stoll in Cahill 301 and be sure to keep the receipt from the vendor. If you haven't ordered for postdoc lunches before, please give Sheri your Caltech ID number, phone number, citizenship status, and mailing address (or direct deposit information) so she can get you your monies.
  • The food should usually be ordered around 11am so that it arrives at 12pm. When giving the location for the drop-off, you can either tell them “the Cahill building at Caltech” or give the address, which is 1216 E. California Blvd. Don’t forget to give them the room number (Cahill 312), too.
  • The food costs should be under $140 including service/delivery/tax! The attendance varies but usually 10-12 postdocs show up on a given week.
  • Please order some vegan dishes to be considerate of people who don’t eat meat or animal byproducts. We have vegan group members, please do this.
  • The person ordering lunch is responsible for wiping down the tables and putting the trash outside the door so we don't stink out the conference room. Don't worry, though -- I and others will usually stay and help.
